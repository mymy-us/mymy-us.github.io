Saying ¡Hola!

Who is that?

I’m Tim, a Lean Manufacturing guy.

Today’s world is a rapid and chaotic one. I’ve walked the talk. My path wasn’t a straight line, and I have navigated various segments of the journey toward achieving lean:

  1. The Start: Interned as a Lean Engineer at a doll company.
  2. The Then: Worked as a Design/Project Quality Engineer in the automotive industry.
  3. The Now: Work as an Industrial Engineer.

Outside the cubicle, I lift heavy at the gym (so I ain’t a skinny biatch) and I’m currently learning Spanish.

Why this blog exists?

The transition from “Academic Theory” to “Corporate Reality” is painful.

New grads’ brain is full of textbook definitions, but the real world runs on messy compromises.

  • School knowledge often doesn’t connect.
  • Corporate Mentors sometimes gatekeep insights to protect their jobs.
  • Coworkers are often too busy to explain the “why.”

I’ve been through the pain. So I’m here to share the experiences and you don’t have to learn the hard way.

My philosophy is simple:  There is nothing to hide. We see further only because we stand on the shoulders of giants.

What you get here?

Think of this as the “No Bullshit Edition” of your degree.

  • The Bridge: Connecting abstract academy concepts to dirty industry practices.
  • The Brevity: Concise posts. No fluff. I respect everyone’s time.
  • The Dialogue: A genuine exchange of ideas.

Ready to cut through the noise? Now, LET’S GO “MY, OH MY!”
